  1. Skeletal Muscle Pump ‚Äì Contraction squeezes vessels
  2. Respiratory Pump ‚Äì Pressure changes in thorax/abdomen
  3. Pulse Pressure ‚Äì Adjacent arteries compress vessels
  4. Smooth Muscle ‚Äì Intrinsic contraction when stretched
  5. One-Way Valves ‚Äì Ensure directional flow

Mature: Thymus
Function: Cell-mediated immunity. Direct attack.
Antigen-specific

B-Cells (10-15%)

Function: Humoral immunity ‚Üí antibodies
Antigen-specific

NK Cells (5-10%)

Mature: Red Bone Marrow
Function: Innate immunity. Viruses + cancer.
NOT antigen-specific

Lymphopoiesis

Production from hematopoietic stem cells in red bone marrow:

  • Some stay in marrow ‚Üí B-cells and NK cells
  • Some migrate to thymus ‚Üí T-cells
  • Memory cells: last 4-20+ years

Lymphatic Organs (Encapsulated)

1. Lymph Nodes: The Filters

Function: Cleanse lymph, activate T & B-cells

Structure:

  • CT capsule with trabeculae
  • Hilus: blood vessels + efferent exit
  • Afferent enter on convex side (more inlets = pressure!)

Flow: Afferent ‚Üí subcapsular sinus ‚Üí cortex (B-cells) ‚Üí paracortex (T-cells) ‚Üí medulla ‚Üí efferent

[Lymph Node Structure]
Cortex ¬∑ Paracortex ¬∑ Medulla

Major clusters: Cervical ¬∑ Axillary ¬∑ Inguinal ¬∑ Mesenteric ¬∑ Tracheobronchial

‚ö†Ô∏è "Swollen glands" = swollen LYMPH NODES (lymphadenitis)

2. Thymus: The Training Ground

Location: Superior mediastinum, above heart

Function: T-cell maturation

Active: Children up to age 2, then involution

Structure: Capsule ‚Üí septae ‚Üí lobules (cortex + medulla)

Hormones: Thymosin, thymopoietin, thymulin

[Thymus Lobule]
Blood-thymus barrier

3. Spleen: The Blood Filter

Location: Left side, lateral to stomach

Functions:

  • Filters blood (removes old RBCs, debris)
  • Immune response to blood antigens
  • Recycles iron (Fe++)
  • Stores platelets
  • Hematopoiesis in fetus (can resume in severe anemia)
[Spleen]
White pulp ¬∑ Red pulp

White pulp: Lymphocytes around arteries (immune surveillance)
Red pulp: Venous sinusoids + cords (RBC filtration)

Pathway: Splenic artery ‚Üí trabecular arteries ‚Üí central arteries (white pulp) ‚Üí capillaries ‚Üí red pulp ‚Üí trabecular veins ‚Üí splenic vein

‚ö†Ô∏è Enlargement = splenomegaly. Removal = splenectomy. Can live without it but higher infection risk.
